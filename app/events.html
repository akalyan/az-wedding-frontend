The wedding weekend will have a lot going on, so please read below for all the details for the weekend.

30August, 2014 - Saturday

  • 12:00

    Pithi & Mehndi Bridal Shower

    530 Broadway E, Seattle, WA

    This family event will be ladies only. The Pithi Ceremony is a traditional blessing and cleansing for the bride before the wedding. The ceremony originated with the long-known ayurvedic benefits of turmeric and other medical properties of the herbal ingredients in the mixture that is spread on the bride’s skin. The mixture is said to infuse a natural glow and ready the bride for the wedding celebrations. The ceremony is a celebration of love and happiness for the bride and will make up a part of Zyanya’s bridal shower. Brunch will be served and after the ceremony a mehndi artist will join us to adorn anyone who wishes with mehndi (henna).

31August, 2014 - Sunday

  • 10:00

    Santhak Ceremony

    The Irving’s Home, Mercer Island

    This family ceremony prays for blessing from the divine for a peaceful wedding ceremony. Breakfast will be served.

  • 18:00

    Raas Garba / Rehearsal Dinner

    1101 Alaskan Way, Seattle, WA

    Out-of-town family and friends are invited for dinner and dancing. Dinner will be served and dancing will begin shortly after. Dress: Semi-Formal, Indian fashion encouraged! Please bring a warm jacket.

1September, 2014 - Monday

  • 9:00

    Baraat

    870 11th Ave E, Seattle, WA

    You won’t want to miss this. Everyone is invited to join Atish’s baraat procession as he enters Volunteer Park to reach the bridal party. Traditionally the baraat was the groom’s procession to the bride’s village where the wedding ceremony would take place. Wear comfortable shoes and come very ready to dance!

    Atish’s baraat will begin next to Lowell Elementary School in a privately reserved parking lot on 11th Ave & Aloha St. The baraat will move North on foot on 11th Volunteer Park, entering the park and continuing around the old water reservoir to the front of the Seattle Asian Art Museum. Here the bride and groom’s parties will meet to perform a brief blessing of the groom by the bride’s family. From here everyone will head to the Dahlia garden for the wedding ceremony.

    (We recommend arriving early to find parking. The parking lot will be available and if full there will be parking in Volunteer Park (4 blocks away) or on the neighborhood streets. Because of our time restrictions in the park the baraat will leave promptly from the parking lot at 9:00am.)

  • 10:00

    Ceremony

    The Dahlia Garden, Volunteer Park Rd, Seattle, WA

    The wedding ceremony will take place outside in the park directly following the baraat. The ceremony will last approximately 1.5 hours. Guests are welcome to mingle and get up for refreshments throughout the ceremony. A light lunch will be served after. Dress: Picnic Formal/Festive. Wear shoes that can be worn in grass.

  • 13:00

    Nap Time

    Your accommodations

    We strongly encourage everyone to rest up and nap before the reception.

  • 18:00

    Reception

    Sodo Park, Seattle

    Come ready to dance the night away! Dinner will be served. Parking is readily available around the building. Taxi’s will be available to bring guests back to their hotel. Events will begin at 6pm. No box gifts please. Dress: Cocktail Formal & danceable shoes!
