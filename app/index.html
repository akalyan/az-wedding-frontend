A tale of salsa, HTML, and love

Once upon a time…

A boy named Atish moved to a new city where the trees stay green year-round and friendly locals hang out in cafés drinking coffee. In this city lived a girl named Zyanya. She was a university student with a passion for dance. She taught dance classes to people that had never danced before.

One day Atish decided it was time to try something new. He wanted to learn how to Salsa. He grabbed a friend and headed to this cool new dance school he had heard about. Meanwhile, the Salsa class was about to start and Zyanya was worried because there were not enough guys in the class to lead all the ladies. She was trying to figure out what to do when two cute guys walked into the studio asking if they could join the class. “Of course!” she said, happy to have the unexpected students.

The class was a success and everyone went home excited to learn more. The next week the class returned for another lesson, but before they began Zyanya had an announcement. The school was growing, she said, and she was looking for someone to teach her HTML so she could manage the new website. Would any Seattle techy be interested in trading salsa lessons for HTML lessons? Atish raised his hand the highest, and so it was meant to be.

Zyanya and Atish met at a café to write some code but the wi-fi was not working so they chatted about work, school, and life. One month later (5 years ago), Atish asked Zyanya to accompany him to a Broadway show and dinner. And the rest, as they say, is history.
